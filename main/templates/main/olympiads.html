Olympiads

This page lists the books, handouts, and references I used during my Math Olympiad preparation. Alongside that, I’d like to share a piece of advice I learned the hard way — by making the mistake myself.

  • Never be afraid to try hard problems — and don’t give up on them too easily.
    I realized this during the final months of my prep, when my problem-solving ability seemed to hit a ceiling. Trying harder problems exposes you to new ideas, unexpected tools, and alternate ways of thinking — even if you don’t solve them. Configuration geometry is a great example: struggling with difficult setups teaches you what kinds of perturbations or tricks are worth exploring later.

    It’s worth mentioning that if you’re in OTIS, try starting directly with the D-version of a unit instead of going through the B-version first. Even if you can’t solve the problems initially, analyze the solutions carefully and go through the examples thoroughly. After all, we humans are nothing but machine-learning models — with an additional parameter for emotions added in. The more you see, the more you learn, and the more you can deliver.
